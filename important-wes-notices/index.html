Delivery Service Suspensions in Ukraine, Russia, and Belarus

March 29, 2022

Ukraine Update

As the war in Ukraine continues, the situation remains dynamic. WES is currently in contact with the Information and Image Center (ENIC Ukraine) of the Ministry of Education and Science of Ukraine. WES is working with ENIC to ensure continued evaluation of Ukrainian educational credentials through standard procedures. Please visit our web site for more information: https://applications.wes.org/required-documents.

For applicants whose circumstances do not permit access to standard procedures, WES provides the Gateway Program in the United States or Canada.

The WES Gateway Program in the United States or Canada  serves applicants with partial documentation of academic accomplishment at Ukrainian institutions who are seeking an evaluation report for education, licensure, or employment purposes, and who have been displaced as a result of adverse circumstances in their country. Applicants with partial documentation who are seeking an evaluation report for immigration to Canada are advised to contact the Government of Canada’s website or learn more about the measures for people affected by the situation in Ukraine.

March 16, 2022

Policy Update for Ukraine

WES is currently unable to verify academic documents with the Information and Image Center (ENIC Ukraine) of the Ukrainian Ministry of Education and Science. Individuals seeking an evaluation report for education, licensure or employment purposes may be eligible for the WES Gateway Program in the United States or Canada. The program enables assessment of the educational credentials of individuals who have been displaced as a result of adverse circumstances in their country and have limited proof of their academic achievements. We invite you to visit the program website to learn more about the eligibility criteria.

Applicants seeking an evaluation report for immigration to Canada are advised to contact the Government of Canada’s website or learn more about the measures for people affected by the situation in Ukraine.

May 20, 2021

Academic Documents from India

WES recognizes the current difficulties that our applicants are facing as they attempt to have documents sent from institutions affected by the pandemic’s resurgence in India.

Here are the steps you can take: 

  1. Call or email your institution. Though many campuses are closed, registrars and other officials are working remotely. Understandably, some institutions are experiencing delays. You might need to reach out several times until you reach someone who will process your request. 
  2. Learn if your institution has an electronic transmission partnership with WES. Over 10,000 institutions worldwide, including many in India, are currently using this method to securely send documents to WES. If yours is one of them, please request that they use this method. (Please note: This is the only way that institutions can send your documents by email.)
  3. If your institution does not have an electronic transmission partnership, please ask them to initiate one. They can use our Contact Us form, and we will be in touch quickly to discuss options.
  4. If your institution has already mailed your documents, no further action is needed at this time. Please be aware that we are mindful of delays caused by the pandemic and we are doing our best to communicate with report recipients on behalf of our affected applicants. As soon as your documents arrive, you will see a status update in My Account.

Our thoughts are with those who have been impacted by the crisis. If you need additional assistance, please Contact Us.

(Update: Mail service has resumed throughout the region.)

Important Updates

World Education Services remains open for business. We are continuing to accept applications and process documents.

We are closely monitoring the global resurgence of COVID-19, particularly its impact on mail and courier services, and taking steps to mitigate any potential delays.

Applicants: 

If you are concerned that your report might be affected, you can:

  • Visit Required Documents to find out if the institution that is providing your academic documents can send documents digitally to WES. If they have not already established secure file transfer protocols with us, please email the institution and request that they contact WES. We will quickly work with them to find a suitable electronic document transmission method, which will expedite the completion of your report.
  • Contact your recipient institutions. In case the pandemic causes any mail delays, ask if they will temporarily accept an unofficial copy of your credential evaluation report. Many institutions in the U.S. and Canada are willing to review the digital version that is sent to your online account before they receive the official, printed report by mail.
  • You can also track the pandemic’s impact on mail and courier services via Canada Post, DHL, FedEx, and Purolator.

Institutions: 

If you receive evaluation reports, there are actions you can take as well. Please consider:

  • Accepting unofficial PDFs as temporary proof of equivalency until you receive official, printed evaluation reports from WES.
  • Creating an AccessWES account to begin receiving e-reports.

Upload Your Degree Certificates and Translations

Most applicants can now upload degree certificates and document translations from the My Account page. Once you log in, go to your Account Timeline and scroll to the Upload Your Documents section, then click the Upload Document button to begin. Please note the following requirements before uploading your documents:

  • Upload only PDF or JPEG files.
  • The maximum file size is 10 MB.
  • Do not password-protect the PDF.
  • Take a moment to ensure you uploaded the correct document and that they are clear, high-quality images.
  • Be sure to preview all your documents before clicking the Submit button. Once you submit your documents, you cannot delete them.

Note: Your account status may not change after you upload your files. We will review your certificates and translations only after we receive your official academic transcripts via postal mail or electronic transmission.
